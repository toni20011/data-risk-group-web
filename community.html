Cultivating
Connections.

Business growth runs on relationships. With the right peers, the right partners, and the right clients. Cultivating Connections is a series of events and experiences designed to help small business leaders build all three.

In-person & virtual Local communities Co-hosted with partners Washington DC · DMV area

Vision

A business environment where small businesses grow because they are genuinely trusted: by their clients, their communities, and the partners they work alongside.

Mission

To create spaces where small business owners gain the awareness, knowledge, and skills to build lasting relationships: with each other and with the clients they serve.

What It Is

Built around two kinds
of connection that drive growth.

Most networking events put people in a room and hope for the best. Cultivating Connections is different. Each event is shaped around a specific community, a specific purpose, and a specific outcome for the people in it.

01

Connect with other business leaders and partners

Structured spaces to meet peers, collaborators, and potential partners who understand what it means to run a small business. Not a card-swap. A real exchange with people who could change your trajectory.

02

Build skills to cultivate relationships with your own clients

Trust is the skill behind every client relationship that holds. Events bring in experts who help you understand what your clients are actually looking for, and how to show up in a way that makes them stay.

How It Works

Built with partners,
not just for them.

Every Cultivating Connections event is designed with a co-host or partner whose community and expertise shapes the room. We bring the framework. Partners bring their people and their perspective.

Co-hosts

Business owners and community leaders

People who already have a trusted audience and want to bring something genuinely useful to them. Share the stage, shape the event.

Expert contributors

Specialists and practitioners

Lawyers, strategists, marketers, technologists. People with a specific lens who want to meet small business owners where they are.

Community sponsors

Business groups and organizations

Chambers of commerce, trade associations, local business networks, and expo partners looking to bring their members something tangible.
